Eggplant Caponata

Eggplant caponata is a delicious relish or appetizer that can be served hot or cold! Serve with toasted crusty bread.

Time for preparing

  • Prep Time: 30 mins
  • Cook Time: 35 mins
  • Additional Time: 30 min
  • Total Time: 1 hrs 35 min
  • Servings: 16

Ingredients

  1. 1 eggplant, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  2. salt to taste
  3. ¼ cup olive oil, divided
  4. 1 cup finely chopped celery
  5. 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  6. 1 clove garlic, minced
  7. 1 ½ cups canned plum tomatoes, drained and coarsely chopped
  8. 12 green olives, pitted and coarsely chopped
  9. 1 ½ tablespoons drained capers
  10. 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  11. 1 teaspoon minced oregano
  12. 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  13. 2 teaspoons white sugar
  14. 1 teaspoon salt
  15. ground black pepper to taste
  16. 2 teaspoons minced fresh parsley, or to taste

Directions

Step 1

Toss eggplant with salt and place in a colander set over a bowl. Let sit, about 30 minutes. Rinse and pat dry.

Step 2

Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add celery; cook, stirring often, until softened, about 4 minutes. Add onion and garlic; cook and stir until onion is soft and lightly golden, about 5 minutes. Transfer the mixture to a bowl using a slotted spoon.

Step 3

Heat remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil in the skillet. Add eggplant and cook, stirring constantly, until lightly browned, 5 to 7 minutes. Stir in celery mixture, tomatoes, olives, capers, tomato paste, and oregano. Bring to a boil; reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered until caponata is thickened, about 15 minutes.

Step 4

Season caponata with vinegar, sugar, salt, and black pepper. Transfer to a serving bowl and garnish with parsley.
